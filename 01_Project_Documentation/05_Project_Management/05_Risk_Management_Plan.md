# 1. Purpose

This document defines the risk management approach for the Professional Portfolio Website.

It establishes the process for identifying, analysing, monitoring, responding to, and documenting project risks throughout the software development lifecycle.

Risk management supports informed decision-making and helps minimise the likelihood and impact of events that could affect project success.

---

# 2. Objectives

The Risk Management Plan aims to:

- Identify project risks early.
- Assess risk likelihood and impact.
- Reduce project uncertainty.
- Protect project quality.
- Support informed decisions.
- Improve project resilience.
- Ensure continuous risk monitoring.

---

# 3. Risk Management Principles

Risk management within this project follows these principles:

- Early identification.
- Continuous monitoring.
- Evidence-based assessment.
- Proactive mitigation.
- Clear ownership.
- Transparent reporting.
- Continuous improvement.

Risk management is an ongoing activity throughout the project lifecycle.

---

# 4. Risk Management Process

The project follows the process below:

1. Identify risks.
2. Analyse likelihood and impact.
3. Prioritise risks.
4. Define response strategies.
5. Assign ownership.
6. Monitor risks.
7. Review effectiveness.
8. Update the Risk Register.

---

# 5. Risk Categories

Project risks may arise from several areas:

## Technical Risks

Examples:

- Architecture changes.
- Software defects.
- Performance issues.
- Browser compatibility.
- Dependency conflicts.

---

## Project Risks

Examples:

- Schedule delays.
- Scope changes.
- Documentation gaps.
- Resource constraints.

---

## Quality Risks

Examples:

- Inconsistent documentation.
- Accessibility issues.
- UI inconsistencies.

---

## Operational Risks

Examples:

- Version control mistakes.
- Accidental file deletion.
- Deployment failures.
- Configuration errors.

---

## External Risks

Examples:

- Third-party service changes.
- GitHub availability.
- Browser updates.
- Internet connectivity issues.

---

# 6. Risk Assessment

Each identified risk should be evaluated using:

### Likelihood

- Low
- Medium
- High

### Impact

- Low
- Medium
- High

The combination of likelihood and impact determines the overall risk priority.

---

# 7. Risk Response Strategies

The project adopts the following response strategies:

### Avoid

Eliminate the risk by changing the approach.

---

### Mitigate

Reduce the likelihood or impact.

---

### Transfer

Shift responsibility where appropriate.

---

### Accept

Acknowledge the risk and monitor it without immediate action.

The selected strategy should be appropriate to the nature of the risk.

---

---

# 9. Risk Monitoring

Project risks should be monitored:

- At the completion of each phase.
- Before implementation.
- Before testing.
- Before deployment.
- After significant project changes.

Risk reviews should be documented.

---

# 10. Escalation

A risk should be escalated when:

- Its likelihood increases significantly.
- Its impact becomes unacceptable.
- Existing mitigation is ineffective.
- It threatens project objectives.

Escalation decisions should follow the Project Governance process.
