# 7. Tone of Voice

## 7.1 Purpose

This document defines the communication style of the Professional Portfolio Website. It ensures that every page, project description, article, and professional interaction communicates with a consistent voice that reflects Emmanuel Chukwuka Origbo's personal brand and professional values.

---

# 7.2 Tone of Voice Statement

The Professional Portfolio Website communicates with clarity, professionalism, confidence, and authenticity. The tone is informative and approachable, demonstrating technical competence without unnecessary complexity or exaggerated claims.

The objective is to help visitors understand both the solutions developed and the thinking behind them.

---

# 7.3 Primary Communication Principles

The website shall communicate in a manner that is:

- Professional
- Clear
- Confident
- Honest
- Respectful
- Informative
- Structured
- Approachable
- Evidence-based

Every statement should support the professional image established throughout the Brand Strategy documents.

---

# 7.4 Writing Style

Content throughout the website should:

- Use simple and precise language.
- Explain technical concepts clearly.
- Focus on practical outcomes.
- Avoid unnecessary technical jargon.
- Organise information logically.
- Support claims with completed work or documented evidence.
- Encourage understanding rather than impress with complexity.

---

# 7.5 Communication Across Different Audiences

### Recruiters and Hiring Managers

Use clear, concise language that highlights skills, experience, achievements, and business impact.

---

### Employers and Business Leaders

Focus on business problems, measurable outcomes, operational improvements, and practical solutions.

---

### Technical Professionals

Provide sufficient technical depth while maintaining readability. Explain methodologies, architectural decisions, technologies, and implementation approaches.

---

### Students and Learners

Communicate in an educational and encouraging manner by explaining concepts, sharing lessons learned, and demonstrating best practices.

---

# 7.6 Language Guidelines

The website should:

- Use active voice whenever appropriate.
- Prefer concise sentences over lengthy paragraphs.
- Define technical terminology where necessary.
- Maintain consistent terminology throughout all pages.
- Avoid slang, exaggerated marketing language, and unsupported claims.

Examples of preferred terminology include:

- Information Systems
- Business Analysis
- Data Analytics
- Analytics Engineering
- Data Engineering
- Software Engineering
- Digital Business Solutions

These terms should be used consistently across the portfolio.

---

# 7.7 Professional Credibility

The website should demonstrate credibility by:

- Presenting factual information.
- Referencing completed projects.
- Showing documented methodologies.
- Acknowledging lessons learned.
- Explaining both successes and challenges.

Professional credibility should come from evidence rather than self-promotion.

---

# 7.8 Consistency Across the Website

The tone of voice should remain consistent across:

- Homepage
- About Page
- Projects
- Case Studies
- Technical Documentation
- Blog Articles (future)
- Contact Page
- GitHub Repository Documentation

Visitors should experience a unified professional voice regardless of which page they visit.

---

# 7.9 Tone of Voice Summary

The Professional Portfolio Website communicates with professionalism, clarity, confidence, and authenticity. By maintaining a consistent and evidence-based communication style, Emmanuel Chukwuka Origbo's portfolio will strengthen trust, improve readability, and reinforce his professional identity across every interaction.
